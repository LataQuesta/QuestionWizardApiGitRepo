Introduction

This report is intended for use by the interviewers of the candidate being assessed. Towards an all-encompassing  unique approach, this report combines the individual’s Personality type with the professional competencies that are critical for success in organizations. Knowing the personality brings out the individual’s preferred style or typical way of behaving that influences their potential performance in the organization. The competency evaluation brings out the behaviours they may have developed over the years as a part of their coping strategy based on feedback or wisdom.

Together it gives us a clear picture of how the candidate views his/her own behaviour, rather than how others might describe them and thus provides a more accurate point of reference for the interviewer to probe and make more informed decisions.

The accuracy of this report depends on the frankness with which the questions were answered as well as the level of self-awareness. The assessment accounts for the overall consistency with which the individual has responded.

While the core personality and hence the Enneagram profiling does not change (proven to be stable and consistent through one’s lifetime), the competency section has a shelf life of 18-24 months. If there are major changes in their life or work, it is recommended that the assessment be taken again.

What can you find in this report?

Enneagram Personality Type – This sections brings out key characteristics and natural styles of the candidate based on their inherent personality. It provides a very clear insight into how they would come across in the organisation and help the assessor to evaluate the alignment towards the role and team

Competency Profile Overview – This section clearly bring out which professional competencies of the individual act as their strengths/ are moderately displayed/ might have become derailers/ are areas that need focus to be developed. The assessor can this information to probe further on critical competencies for the role

Competency Summary – This segment provides an overall scoring on 4 the Competency clusters. It further brings out the comparison of the individual’s responses vis-à-vis the normative scores based on the individual’s personality type. It helps identify areas that can naturally be developed by this individual

Interview Questions – This section focussed on competency-based questions which can be useful to the assessors during the interview

Definitions of the competencies – This segment provides a clear understanding of the behaviours associated with each of the 15 competencies

Profile overview

This section classifies the competencies across a spectrum from strengths to derailers based on the scores of the individual on each of the evaluated professional competencies.


Strength Competencies that the individual may be displaying effectively most often– can be inherent or learned over time.
Balanced Competences where the individual has scored moderately – they may not be consistent in their display of these competencies, but these can be used more effectively if focused upon.
Needs development Competencies which are not displayed significantly, thereby impacting effectiveness and thus requiring focus.
Neglected Competencies that are the most under-used, impacting outcomes and performance and will require effort to develop.
Derailer Competencies that are over-used and can be a detriment to success, even acting as showstoppers; should be moderated to become strengths.
