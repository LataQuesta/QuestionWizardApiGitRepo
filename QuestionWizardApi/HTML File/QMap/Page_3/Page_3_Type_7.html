Enneagram Type: The Versatile Visionary

Core Motivation - To experience life to the fullest and avoid pain

  • Have a mind buzzing with ideas, thoughts, plans and actions
  • Can quickly synthesise information and multi-task
  • Can be impatient and over-extend themselves
  • Can be visionaries who can energise the team and can be good contributors towards achieving the vision of the organization
  • Can be charismatic, innovative and creative
  • Don’t like too many hierarchies and do not want to deal with unpleasant emotions at work
  • Prefer to look at the bigger picture, leaving the tedious task of execution at times to others

What they value in a role/org culture?

What can demotivate them?

  • Cultures that offer lots of freedom and flexibility to do work their way
  • Environments that are dynamic, ever-changing, where they able to think creatively; brainstorm about the future
  • Role/Culture that allows them to wear multiple hats offers fast-paced projects with shorter deadlines
  • Prefer cultures with more focus on people than hierarchies
  • A rigid work environment or overly bureaucratic one where they are micro-managed and given lots of routines, repetitive tasks
  • Where supervisors/bosses are autocratic and domineering, not open to the ideas they may have
  • Slow pace in general, with little connection to people, can easily bring down their motivation levels

Decision Making Style

Interpersonal Style

  • Like to examine possibilities & entertain their options; hence are adept at creating multiple pathways toward the solution
  • Come up with creative ideas during crises
  • When stressed, they can be overly anxious or impulsive about the decisions
  • Resent being rushed and may snap at people who push them to take a call
  • Have a story-telling style which captures people’s attention and influences others
  • They think, talk & do things fast, making it difficult for others to keep up
  • Having a positive outlook makes them upbeat, exciting & a stimulating company
  • They like to relate to people, engage in enjoyable activities and help others visualise the future in an imaginative way

Conflict Management Style

Managerial Style

  • To avoid feeling unpleasant,they may totally avoid conflicts or may seek shortcuts
  • May find it hard to focus on the other person’s story
  • Usually, may retreat first, then become demanding
  • May make exaggerated commitments which may end up in poor follow-through
  • Can make the workplace fun and encourage team members to find fun at work
  • Team members get along well with them, as long as they don’t cause problems, create negativity or limit their freedom
  • When stressed, they can be unfocused, leaving the team members to fend for themselves, or switch course without notice
