Enneagram Type: The Perceptive Specialist

Core Motivation - To understand and to make sense of the world

  • Are smart, curious, intense, and want to gain more knowledge
  • Are independent and self-sufficient
  • Have a rational approach and a clear analytical mind – focused on problem-solving, data gathering and analysis of details
  • Seem quiet and aloof but can be very talkative when it is about their subject matter
  • Like to research and explore areas that are unusual and unthinkable
  • Need to be an expert in at least one area and that expertise is their way of connecting with the world

What they value in a role/org culture?

What can demotivate them?

  • Cultures that allow well balanced objective analysis to solve complex problems
  • Cultures that are research and innovation-oriented, foster independence and private time,and focus on predictable empirical data and facts
  • Where there are clearly defined boundaries and autonomy to do things their way
  • An organisation/role that requires excessive reliance on collaboration and team/group work
  • When decisions are based on emotions, random arguments or incomplete analysis
  • Too many social obligations or commitments
  • Too many surprises or unclear work parameters

Decision Making Style

Interpersonal Style

  • Logically analyse & organise options assessing the pros & cons of each possibility; assess the relevant facts and make decisions that are not impulse-driven
  • At times, may struggle with analysis-paralysis – wanting more information and time to be comfortable with the decision
  • When stressed, may feel uncertain about everything, even doubting their skill
  • Are more of observers than participants in conversations
  • Like to connect with others at an intellectual level, disconnecting from emotions, both theirs and others
  • Do not like to engage in small talk or share information about themselves
  • Don’t like interruptions, intrusion or surprises or people who are needy or emotional

Conflict Management Style

Managerial Style

  • Lack of opposition - can be confused with support by others
  • Can make decisions without apparent input from others
  • Can be detached and unemotional when faced with conflict
  • May be uncomfortable with others emotions during conflict
  • Establish work boundaries
  • Are thoughtful, understanding & open-minded
  • Respect those who think through problems and structure effective work processes
  • Are straightforward and professional
  • Are not emotionally reactive
  • When stressed, can be uncommunicative & remote; may not connect as much; may come across as uncaring, aloof & arrogant
