Enneagram Type: The Intense Individualist

Core Motivation - To be unique and authentic

  • Connect and understand people well
  • Can be persuasive and skilled at drawing people out
  • Want to feel unique or different and hence are highly innovative
  • Can be creative and reflective and focused on aesthetics/beauty
  • Bring in a lot of passion and dedication
  • Are well connected to a range of emotions - both happy and sad
  • Can come across as moody and withdrawn
  • They are highly empathetic and sensitive to the needs of those around them

What they value in a role/org culture?

What can demotivate them?

  • Cultures that encourage expression of individuality and authenticity
  • Where there is freedom and flexibility, innovative and creative solutions/idea can be generated
  • Where they can influence stakeholders and make meaningful contributions that are highly valued
  • A rigid, formal environment where status quo and routine are valued over innovation and creativity
  • Where the bulk load of the work includes too many routine tasks that require following detailed structures or process
  • An environment where they have to deal with rejection constantly

Decision Making Style

Interpersonal Style

  • When taking a decision are guided by their values and are usually self-aware and compassionate to themselves & others
  • When they feel their values are being compromised, they may take a long time to make the decisions
  • When stressed, they may withdraw & make decisions purely based on their feelings
  • Can appear warm & empathic yet flat and pessimistic
  • Are unafraid to share the darker, deeper or melancholy aspects of life with others
  • Their 'self-referencing' style of talking may prevent them from seeing what is happening around them
  • Have a strong opinion & can offer deep insights

Conflict Management Style

Managerial Style

  • May adopt a push-pull style of relating at times of conflict
  • Can be harsh towards their competitors and adversaries
  • Insist that colleagues choose sides
  • Their need to process thoughts and feelings may lead to hesitation and initial withdrawal from the conflict
  • Make good personal connections with those they work with & are emotionally available, especially when their team is struggling
  • Are open to honest inputs from their teams
  • Are deeply appreciative of their contributions
  • When stressed, may become withdrawn and see everything through a negative lens or become angry and demanding
