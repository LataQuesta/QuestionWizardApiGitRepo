Enneagram Type: The Dutiful Loyalist

Core Motivation - To be safe and secure hence focus goes to reading people and situations, to determine how trustworthy and safe they are.

  • Are engaging, warm and likeable
  • Seek comfort and security
  • Focus is on the worst-case scenario or possible threats
  • Ask very critical and important questions that help cover bases
  • Have a very logical mind and can be a very good judge of character
  • Are loyal employees/colleagues and stand by others during times of crisis
  • Can be self-doubting and anxious

What they value in a role/org culture?

What can cause disengagement?

  • Seek stability and predictability
  • Seek transparent processes & structures; roles and responsibilities
  • Cultures that bring people together, build collaboration, co-operation, and support
  • An environment where they feel safe, secure, and can trust people
  • Constantly changing expectations
  • Uncertainty and unpredictability; unclear deliverables
  • A very competitive environment where collaboration and trust are secondary
  • Where feedback and inputs aren't valued and concerns are not heard

Decision Making Style

Interpersonal Style

  • Focus is on finding the most secure yet practical solution that is risk-free
  • Think through the impact of all possible scenarios considering future hurdles and roadblocks when making decisions
  • When stressed, they may get into analysis paralysis mode, often choosing the safest course of action
  • Easy-going, warm and approachable
  • Go along with the rules and comply
  • Maybe cautious and sceptical at times
  • Prefer to build bonds of trust
  • Speech is indicative of their tentativeness; use words like maybe, possible, perhaps
  • Seek additional reassurance by asking questions

Conflict Management Style

Leadership Style

  • Places emphasis on collective decisioning - discussion & collaboration
  • Most likely to seek consensus and comply with the group decision
  • May become impulsive and reactive, defending themselves and their decision
  • Want alignment with the group - may see disagreement as being attacked
  • Are honest, trustworthy & humble, worrying for themselves and their teams
  • Throw caution to the wind if they feel things are going down the wrong path
  • If they don't agree with what they are doing, may call out to the authorities - question them and speak the truth to power
  • Take a long time to develop trust and it can be irritating for others to continually feel like they are testing and doubting them
