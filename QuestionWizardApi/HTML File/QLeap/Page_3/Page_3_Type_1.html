Enneagram Type : The Ethical Perfectionist

Core Motivation - To be good/to do the right thing hence focus goes to the gap between how something is and to what it could be and what would be required to bridge the gap.

  • Are objective, logical and a good problem solver
  • Good creators of structures, systems, and processes
  • Demonstrate detail consciousness and go the extra mile to avoid making any mistakes
  • Have a clarity of purpose and a strong sense of responsibility
  • Are highly principled and rule-abiding
  • Show a great deal of discipline and self-control to work towards a goal
  • Are more focused on the task at hand rather than emotions

What they value in a role/org culture?

What can cause disengagement

  • Having clear guidelines, structures, rules and regulations to follow
  • Well-defined accountability and responsibility matrices
  • A larger purpose to connect with and a long-term vision
  • Where they are able to influence standards and drive quality
  • Leaders or co-workers who are unethical
  • Situations/conditions where there is a lack of consistent direction and they are unable to see how things can get better
  • Leaders/co-workers who ignore feedback and ideas
  • They are labelled as critical for paying attention to detail

Decision Making Style

Interpersonal Style

  • Focus is on responsibility, common sense, obligations & issues of right or wrong
  • Their quick and efficient decision-making style can prevent them from considering a variety of alternate factors
  • Can be rigid and demanding
  • Can operationalise decisions quickly
  • May focus on perfecting others
  • Can be more demanding of others
  • Can be the teachers/mentors, causing others to see them as superior
  • At times can be gentle, benevolent, funny, and appreciative of people's efforts

Conflict Management Style

Leadership Style

  • They bring in a structured, defined & methodical process apart from bringing in data to resolve conflict with others
  • The motto is "we must be logical about this" & "do the right thing the right way"
  • May project impatience and judgement
  • Their feedback may not seem as constructive even when it is well-intentioned
  • Bring great work ethics, try hard to do their best in everything
  • Can be objective & diligent problem solvers
  • Ensure that expectations, instructions, and communication with the team members are precise and well-planned out
  • They can be rigid and inflexible when it comes to following rules and adhering to proper procedures
