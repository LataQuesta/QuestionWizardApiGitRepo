Enneagram Type: The Charismatic Controller

Core Motivation - To be strong and in control/charge hence focus goes to the big-picture, to what they think should happen and how to make that vision a reality.

  • Highly action-oriented; they take charge of the situation and love to take control of their life and others around them
  • Highly self-confident and self-assured
  • Can see the big picture & help others see it too
  • Are assertive, blunt, straightforward & direct
  • Can be depended on to take decisive action
  • Have a can-do attitude and are unafraid of conflicts
  • Like to make big changes happen but are also able to create a shared vision for their team

What they value in a role/org culture?

What can cause disengagement?

  • Cultures that are energetic & pace-setting & value decisive action
  • Where they can leave a mark & make a significant impact on the business
  • Where they have autonomy over their part of the universe
  • Where others can be respected for their competence and are ready to share their opinions when the situation arises
  • Cultures that offer overly prescriptive expectations about behaviour & minimal flexibility to manage one's domain
  • A large part of their work includes boring, monotonous, and routine work that doesn't allow them to challenge their capabilities
  • Lack of operational freedom and high level of micro-management

Decision Making Style

Interpersonal Style

  • Dislike dilly-dallying around decisions
  • Are decisive & direct - depend on their gut; are focused on big action & have a system's understanding
  • Keep in mind that their freedom doesn't get taken away when making a decision
  • When stressed, may be impulsive & reckless - taking up the most appealing option without taking time to think through
  • Don't like beating around the bush, they are direct & expect the same from others
  • Bring a pragmatic and logical perspective to conversations and issues
  • Can get bossy when stressed or when things go wrong
  • Aren't afraid to voice their strong opinion or push for what they want; can intimidate others

Conflict Management Style

Leadership Style

  • Often can act too quickly, without considering others' feelings
  • See life as a battlefield with enemies everywhere
  • Can push the limits for clarity
  • Can be seen as aggressive complainers
  • State their agenda but do not listen to others
  • Lead with confidence and passion, inspiring their teams and colleagues
  • Not afraid of conflicts, before taking a decision prefer having productive debates
  • Delegate well when they trust their teams competence, which may give them the freedom to act independently
  • May get so focused on moving things forward that they 'steamroll' people who get in their way
