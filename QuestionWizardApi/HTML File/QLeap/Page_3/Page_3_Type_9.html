Enneagram Type: Receptive Peacemaker

Core Motivation - To be settled and in harmony hence focus goes to other people's agendas, go with the flow, support others without drawing attention to themselves.

  • Are pleasant, approachable, and accommodating personalities who seek peace and harmony around them
  • Are kind, understanding and dependable
  • Their decisions can seem delayed as they are focused on taking consensus
  • Tend to avoid conflicts and like to get along with everyone
  • They respect the opinions of those around them without being judgmental
  • They prefer routine and regularity and dislike changes

What they value in a role/org culture?

What can cause disengagement?

  • Cultures that offer a stable, pleasant & congenial environment where they can expect predictability & routine
  • Work at a relaxed pace without being pressured or stressed
  • Offers high collaboration, low conflict teams, giving them the opportunity to collaborate with various departments & stakeholders
  • High conflict, aggressive, competitive environments with a clear winner & loser
  • A very dynamic environment requiring quick changes in plans and directives, having a low predilection for routine
  • Cultures that lack the need for consensus or collaboration; may feel left out and can be triggered if they feel overlooked or ignored

Decision Making Style

Interpersonal Style

  • See many different sides to a situation & empathises with the people concerned
  • Good at unifying all the perspectives involved and getting consensus from others for a harmonious decision
  • Struggle with indecision if unsure about how it will impact others around them
  • Can feel extremely pressured when asked to make controversial or unilateral decisions
  • Are warm approachable & neutral, have a calm tone & use gentle encouraging phrases
  • Are more attuned to the agendas of others than they are to their own
  • Are good at mediating conflict and being diplomatic, being respectful of others and their feelings
  • Make others feel supported and included

Conflict Management Style

Leadership Style

  • Start off as ambivalent and then turn to stubborn non-communication
  • Inadvertently create conflict by withholding information
  • Their non-action strategy can irritate others
  • Have difficulty setting boundaries and may become overwhelmed
  • Are good mediators & facilitators, setting up meetings and structures
  • Are affable, accommodating & easy to be around; ensure everyone is heard
  • Lead through diplomacy, inclusiveness consensus, patience, & respect for others
  • Under stress, can be unassertive, indecisive, conflict avoidant, & overly accommodating
