Enneagram Type : The Empathic Nurturer

Core Motivation - To be liked and appreciated hence focus goes to people around them and how to best forge positive relations by managing people and interactions.

Type-based Strengths
  • Positive Attitude - their positive, constructive, and "we-can-fix-this" attitude means that they are often well-liked and become excellent team players
  • Team-Oriented - have the desire to drive communities together and get people working better as a team
  • Empathetic - have the ability to naturally recognize the needs of others and want to get to know other people
  • Persistent - Â are often persistent and dedicated to any tasks at hand
  • Encouraging - are supportive and encouraging of others around them which, again, makes them an excellent team player

Typical challenges for people who work with them
  • Twos have a hard time being honest and direct about what is not working for them but can vent about it to others which can be seen as complaining
  • Believe that they can do it all themselves but get angry and resentful when others are not helping or supporting them
  • While they constantly try to help, twos do resent lack of appreciation from others
  • In order to not hurt others and please them, twos may overcommit and feel overwhelmed

Under stress they may show the following behaviours
  • Manipulative - when they do not get their way, they may overtly display give-and-take behaviour even resorting to manipulation at times, to get the job done
  • Complaining - can become excessively complaining about things around them, from work to even their personal life
  • Can display bouts of anger and demandingness when they feel that they have been under-appreciated for their contributions for long periods of time
  • Can play the martyr/victim and be guilt-inducing when someone questions them or becomes demanding with them

What could negatively impact their performance
  • Excessive focus on building relationships can at times be at the cost of results
  • Being empathetic towards others may make them lose focus of their own needs making them feel like martyrs
  • Service orientation towards others can be taken to the extreme by being self-sacrificial, leading to victim behaviour
  • Are demonstrative, but when they don't know when to stop, it may lead to being effusive and intrusive
