Introduction

This report has been created with the intention of providing additional information to the interviewers about the candidate being assessed. Towards a unique all-encompassing approach, this report combines the candidate's personality type with the leadership competencies that are critical for success in teams and organisations. Knowing the personality brings out the individual's preferred style or typical way of behaving that influences their potential performance and how they help build/sustain the organisation's culture. The competency evaluation brings out the behaviours they may have developed over the years through feedback or wisdom as part of their coping strategy. Together it gives us a clear picture of how the individual views his/her behaviour, rather than how others might describe them and thus provides a more accurate point of reference for the interviewer to probe and make more informed decisions.

The accuracy of this report depends on the frankness with which the questions were answered as well as the level of self-awareness. The assessment accounts for the overall consistency with which the individual has responded. While the core personality and hence the Enneagram profiling does not change (proven to be stable and consistent through one's lifetime), the competency section has a shelf life of 18-24 months. If there are significant changes in their life or work, it is recommended that the assessment be retaken.

What can you find in this report?

Enneagram Personality Type – This section brings out the natural style and the key characteristics of the candidate based on their inherent personality providing a clear insight into how they would come across in the workplace. It helps the assessors evaluate the candidate’s alignment with the role, team and organisation.

Competency Profile Overview – Based on extensive research, 18 leadership competencies have been identified as critical to success and growth in organisations. This section clearly brings out which of these competencies act as strengths/ are moderately displayed/ might have become derailers/ are areas that need focus to be developed by the individual. The assessors can use this information to further probe competencies that are critical for the role.

Competency Summary - This segment provides an overall scoring on the 5  Competency clusters under which the Leadership competencies are bifurcated. It further brings out the comparison of the individual’s responses vis-à-vis the normative scores based on the individual’s personality type. It helps identify areas that can naturally be developed by this individual.

Interview Questions – This section focuses on competency-based questions which can be useful to the assessors during the interview

Definitions of the competencies – This segment provides a clear understanding of the behaviours associated with each of the 18 competencies

Profile overview

This section classifies each of the leadership competencies across a spectrum from strengths to derailers based on the scores of the individual.

Strength Competencies that the individual may be displaying effectively most often - can be inherent or learned over time
Balanced Moderately scored competencies. May not be displayed consistently and can be developed into strengths if focused upon
Needs development Competencies which are not displayed significantly, thereby impacting effectiveness and thus requiring focus
Neglected Competencies that are the most under-used, impacting outcomes and performance and will require effort to develop
Derailer Competencies that are over-used and can be a detriment to success, even acting as showstoppers; should be moderated to become strengths
