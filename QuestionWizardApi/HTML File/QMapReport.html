Introduction

This report is intended for use by the interviewers of the candidate being assessed. Towards an all-encompassing  unique approach, this report combines the individual’s Personality type with the professional competencies that are critical for success in organizations. Knowing the personality brings out the individual’s preferred style or typical way of behaving that influences their potential performance in the organization. The competency evaluation brings out the behaviours they may have developed over the years as a part of their coping strategy based on feedback or wisdom.

Together it gives us a clear picture of how the candidate views his/her own behaviour, rather than how others might describe them and thus provides a more accurate point of reference for the interviewer to probe and make more informed decisions.

The accuracy of this report depends on the frankness with which the questions were answered as well as the level of self-awareness. The assessment accounts for the overall consistency with which the individual has responded.

While the core personality and hence the Enneagram profiling does not change (proven to be stable and consistent through one’s lifetime), the competency section has a shelf life of 18-24 months. If there are major changes in their life or work, it is recommended that the assessment be taken again.

What can you find in this report?

It provides a very clear insight into how they would come across in the organisation and help the assessor to evaluate the alignment towards the role and team

Competency Profile Overview – This section clearly bring out which professional competencies of the individual act as their strengths/ are moderately displayed/ might have become derailers/ are areas that need focus to be developed. Interview Questions

In order to get a better understanding of the individual and to cross reference the scores on their and responses we have given some questions below to enable the interviewer to probe a little better and understand if what the person has self-reported is also substantiated with examples of such behavioural display.

  • Planning & Organising - How do you plan your week and month at work?

  • Adaptability & Flexibility - Describe a time when your manager made a major process change and you had to implement it in a short time. How did you go about it?

  • Achievement Oriented - If you realize in the quarterly review that a team member has not performed on most parameters, what will you do?

  • Develops Others - If your organization decides to keep no limit on numbers of holidays an employee can take, would you support this policy?

  • Empathy - Which team that has worked under you are you most proud of and why?

Definitions of Competencies

1. Business Acumen

The ability to take a ‘big picture’ view of a situation, to weigh it up quickly, make a logical, sound decision confidently, and influence others to agree with the thoughts, in order to have a positive impact towards achieving the objectives of the organisation.
  • Adaptability & Flexibility - is the ability to be responsive to new information, changing conditions and circumstances, the commitment to making ongoing improvements in systems, processes or ways of working and the ability to handle or make use of uncertain situations effectively.
  • Customer Orientation – is the ability to bring focus onto the needs of the customers first. The competency aligns the business goals with customer goals, incorporates the changing wants and needs of the customer, considers the entire customer journey, sets structures and procedures to instil customer centricity in self and their teams/others.
  • Planning & Organising – is the ability to manage self and/or others, and the resources - including time and surrounding circumstances to reach a specific goal; the ability to effectively set goals, goals, objectives and targets, create work schedules and plans within associated budgets and resources to reach the given objectives.
  • Sustainable Performance – is the ability to replicate positive behaviour and positive performance day after day leading to consistency, dependability and predictability of results and delivery which is comforting to managers, colleagues and customers alike. They focus on continual growth and process improvements, a regular flow of new ideas and innovation.

2. Drive for Results

Refers to the ability to create an outcome and results driven environment by seizing opportunities, raising the bar of achievement, demonstrating motivation, passion and persistence in the face of difficulties and showcasing excellence and expertise in solving problems and decision making
  • Achievement Oriented - is the drive and passion to accomplish goals. They strive to continuously improve their work and be more efficient; set high goals and standards, believe in results and work hard to achieve them. They focus on excelling in all that they do and be successful.
  • Persuading & Influencing – the ability to change the views of others to one's own way of thinking, by engaging through ideas, sharing and growing knowledge and building professional and social connects. This is the ability to influence without force or coercion whilst acknowledging the opinions of others.
  • Problem Solving & Solution Orientation – Is the ability to analyse the situation to produce outside the box solutions, turn creative ideas into original solutions, as well as take risks if there is potential benefit to overcome critical issues and problems.
  • Stakeholder Management – is the ability to communicate and build excellent relationships with various internal and external stakeholders. It involves identifying, analysing and planning and implementing tasks to engage with them and influence them to bring about a favourable outcome or a win-win situation.

Definitions of Competencies

3. Leading & Developing Talent

Refers to the ability to build a culture of investing in people, to develop and coach others constructively, to inspire, motivate, engage and empower people and enable them to maximize their potential.
  • Develops Others - is the ability to coach team members by sharing knowledge, skills, or expertise with them, take action to guide them, broaden their skillset for advancement to greater levels of proficiency and responsibility; someone who steps aside and creates a supporting environment for others to growth and gain success.
  • Empathy – is the ability to recognise and understand the feelings, motives and situations of others and being sensitive to them. Use of empathetic leadership skills, helps them better understand why and how employees react to certain situations and gives them insight into the perceptions of others which is especially critical in times of uncertainty.
  • Promotes teamwork and collaboration – is the preference to share their ideas, skills and perspectives to take people along. This includes understanding a variety of perspectives, managing priorities of everyone in the group, and meeting expectations as a reliable member of the team who is prepared to follow the consensus and norms.

4. Managing Self

Refers to the ability to regulate one’s thoughts, emotions, and actions constructively; motivate oneself, prioritize goals, delay gratification, manage stress, display agility and be accountable for one’s actions; the ability to purposefully manage self, take initiative, set independent goals and actively work towards achieving them even in the VUCA world.
  • Learning Agility & Growth Mindset - Learning Agility is the ability to learn something new in one place and then apply what we learned elsewhere, in a wholly different situation. It is the ability to learn, adapt, unlearn, and relearn to keep up with constantly changing conditions. Individual with growth mindset believe that the most basic abilities can be developed through dedication and hard work—brains and talent are just the starting point.
  • Accountability – is the acceptance of responsibility for honest and ethical conduct towards others. It is also the acceptance of responsibility for one's own actions. It implies a willingness to be transparent, allowing others to observe and evaluate one's performance.
  • Tenacity – is the ability to understand the difference between failure and success and take self and/or their teams from doing ‘all right’ towards ‘thriving’. They are determined to achieve a goal and unwilling to give up when faced with a challenge.
  • Ethics & Integrity – refers to having sound judgement, honesty, dependability, and loyalty. Integrity is based on values rather than personal gain. Such individuals are trustworthy, reliable, practice open and honest communication and are responsible for the tasks assigned to them.
